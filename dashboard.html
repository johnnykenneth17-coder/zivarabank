Security Info

⚠️ Common Banking Scams

Phishing Emails

Scammers send fake emails pretending to be from feecent asking for your login details. Never click links in suspicious emails.

Vishing Calls

Criminals call pretending to be bank officials. They claim there's a problem with your account and ask for your PIN or OTP. feecent will NEVER ask for your PIN.

Smishing (SMS Scams)

Fake text messages with links to fraudulent websites. Always type feecent.com directly into your browser.

Data Breaches

Use unique passwords for different accounts. If one site is breached, criminals will try your password elsewhere.

🔐 Password Security Best Practices

Use a Strong Password
Minimum 8 characters, mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
Don't Reuse Passwords
Each account should have a unique password
Use a Password Manager
Tools like Bitwarden or LastPass can generate and store strong passwords
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Adds an extra layer of security to your account
Regularly Update Your Password
Change your password every 3-6 months

💔 Romance & Love Scams - WARNING

Scammers create fake profiles on dating sites to build emotional connections before asking for money.

Red Flags to Watch For:
  • They profess love quickly without meeting in person
  • They claim to be living or working overseas (military, oil rig, doctor)
  • They have an urgent need for money (medical emergency, travel issues, business problem)
  • They ask for gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers
  • You've never video called with them
What To Do:
  • Never send money to someone you haven't met in person
  • Do a reverse image search of their profile pictures
  • Talk to friends or family about the relationship
  • Report suspicious profiles to the dating platform

👨‍👩‍👧 Family & Relative Scams

Criminals impersonate family members in distress to request emergency funds.

Common Tactics:
  • "Grandparent Scam" - Caller claims to be grandchild needing bail money
  • "Relative in Hospital" - Urgent request for medical bills
  • "Stranded Traveler" - Contact from "family member" stuck abroad needing flight money
  • WhatsApp/Facebook account takeover - Scammer hacks account and messages contacts for money
Protection Tips:
  • Always verify by calling the family member directly on their known number
  • Ask a question only they would know the answer to
  • Never send money to unknown accounts, even if the message seems urgent
  • Enable two-factor authentication on social media accounts
